Dilutions are a high-leverage pattern in life science because the story is authentic, the numbers are realistic, and grading can be made robust with explicit units and tolerances.

Good fits for this pattern

  • Serial dilutions (1:2, 1:10, etc.).
  • Preparing a working concentration from a stock.
  • Standard curve setup questions where the series values matter more than the plot.

What breaks when the content is biology

  • Students mix up dilution factor vs final concentration.
  • Units drift (mL vs μL, mg/mL vs μg/mL) unless the prompt is explicit.
  • Repeating decimals create rounding disputes unless values are constrained.

PGML-first prompt outline

Story: You prepare a 1:10 dilution series from a 2.0 mg/mL stock.
Data: factor, number of tubes, initial concentration.
Task: compute the concentration in tube 4 (mg/mL).
