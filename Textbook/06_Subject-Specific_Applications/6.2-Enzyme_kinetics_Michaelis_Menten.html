Enzyme kinetics prompts can be graded reliably when you keep the question focused: ask for one parameter read or one computed velocity, and make units and tolerances explicit.

Good fits for this pattern

  • Compute v given Vmax, Km, and substrate concentration.
  • Read a parameter from a small table of experimental conditions.
  • Match parameter changes to qualitative effects (MC/MA).

What breaks in practice

  • Students confuse Km with Vmax or treat Km as a rate.
  • Unit mismatch: concentration units differ between Km and [S].
  • Prompts ask for both a computation and an interpretation without separating them.

PGML-first prompt outline

Data: Vmax = 120 nmol/min, Km = 8.0 uM, substrate = 4.0 uM
Task: compute v (nmol/min) to one decimal place
