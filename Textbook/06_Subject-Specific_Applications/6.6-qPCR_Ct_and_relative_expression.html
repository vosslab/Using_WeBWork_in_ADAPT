qPCR questions often fail because Ct is already on a log-like scale and small mistakes become large fold-change errors. Grading works best when you specify the model (doubling per cycle) and the reporting format (Ct, delta Ct, or fold change).

Good fits for this pattern

  • Compute a delta Ct from two Ct values (NUM).
  • Interpret which sample has higher expression (MC) based on Ct values.
  • Multi-part: compute delta Ct and then report fold change under a stated model.

What breaks in practice

  • Students treat a higher Ct as higher expression unless the prompt reminds them.
  • The base of the fold-change model is not stated (2x per cycle vs other efficiencies).
  • Answers differ by a factor of 2^n, causing tolerance-based grading to be meaningless.

PGML-first prompt outline

Data: Ct_control = 18.2, Ct_treatment = 21.2
Model: one cycle difference corresponds to a 2-fold change.
Task: report fold change (treatment relative to control).
