Pathway logic questions work best when you present a small, explicit system and test one inference at a time. Matching and multiple answer items can work well here when distractors reflect common misinterpretations.

Good fits for this pattern

  • Match pathway components to roles (enzyme, regulator, product).
  • Select which perturbations increase or decrease an output (MA).
  • Order a short set of steps in a pathway (ORDER) only when the sequence is truly standard.

What breaks in practice

  • Too many steps: the item becomes a reading test instead of a biology test.
  • Ambiguous arrows: activation vs conversion vs transport without labels.
  • Hidden assumptions about feedback that are not stated.

PGML-first prompt outline

Provide a 4-step pathway with one inhibitor.
Ask: which intervention increases the final product?
