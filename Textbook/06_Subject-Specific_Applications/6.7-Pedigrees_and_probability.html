Pedigree and inheritance probability prompts work well when you keep the model assumptions explicit and keep the computation small. This pattern pairs well with multi-part blanks and a follow-up concept check.

Good fits for this pattern

  • Compute a probability of a genotype given parental genotypes.
  • Compute an expected count given a family size.
  • Multiple choice interpretation: which inheritance model best matches the pattern.

What breaks in practice

  • Prompts imply autosomal vs sex-linked without stating it.
  • Dominance assumptions are unclear or contradicted by the story.
  • Students choose phenotype probabilities when genotype probabilities are requested.

PGML-first prompt outline

Story: autosomal recessive trait, carrier parents.
Task: probability the next child is affected.
