Genotype-to-phenotype mapping is a good place to mix a short calculation with a concept check. The key is to keep naming conventions and interpretation language explicit.

Good fits for this pattern

  • Compute expected genotype frequencies, then select a phenotype statement (MC).
  • Multi-part blanks: one blank for a fraction, one blank for an interpretation term.
  • Matching: alleles or genotypes to phenotype descriptions.

What breaks in practice

  • Ambiguity: "dominant phenotype" without a defined phenotype.
  • Format mismatch: fractions vs decimals; genotype notation styles (Aa vs A/a).
  • Multiple reasonable interpretations when penetrance is implied but not stated.

PGML-first prompt outline

Story: Aa x Aa cross with complete dominance.
Task 1: expected fraction of aa offspring.
Task 2: identify which genotype(s) show the recessive phenotype.
