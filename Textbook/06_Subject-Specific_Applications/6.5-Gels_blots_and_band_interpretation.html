Gel and blot interpretation is a natural fit for structured prompts and ordering questions. These problems become reliable when you separate the data description from the inference being graded.

Good fits for this pattern

  • Order bands by size when the relationship is unambiguous (ORDER).
  • Multiple choice inference: which lane contains the largest fragment, or which sample is most expressed.
  • Matching lane labels to conditions when the mapping is the main skill.

What breaks in practice

  • Students reverse "migrates farther" and "larger" without explicit reminder of direction.
  • Axes or markers are missing, forcing guessing.
  • Images fail to render; prompts should remain solvable with a textual description of key observations.

Prompt outline without relying on images

Provide a small table: lane -> migration distance (mm).
Task: rank fragments from largest to smallest.
