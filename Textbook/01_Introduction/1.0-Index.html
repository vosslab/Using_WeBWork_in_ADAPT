There are plenty of guides for writing mathematics assessment questions in WeBWorK. This guide tries to take a different approach and is written for an audience in the sciences and descriptive fields. Neil Voss, the initial author of this guide, is a professor in life science, though he has a background in mathematics and physics.

What this guide emphasizes

  • PGML-first authoring: write the student-facing prompt in PGML and keep "regular PG" code to the minimum needed to support PGML.
  • Science-friendly prompts: units, significant figures, tabular data, and story problems that match lab and lecture workflows.
  • Practical reuse: patterns you can clone and modify to build question banks quickly and safely.
