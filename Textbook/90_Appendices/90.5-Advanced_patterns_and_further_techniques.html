This section is a curated "further techniques" list for authors who need more than the PGML-first baseline. It intentionally focuses on patterns that are most relevant to life science prompts (tables, strings, custom feedback, and robustness) rather than advanced calculus or graphics.

Patterns worth knowing

  • Strings in context: define a controlled set of acceptable words or labels when free text is necessary.
  • Data tables: present small datasets in an accessible format and compute from them.
  • Custom error messages: make grading feedback clearer when a common wrong interpretation appears repeatedly.
  • Answer hints: provide targeted feedback for one or two common errors without turning the problem into a tutor.
  • Adaptive parameters: adjust later parts of a problem based on earlier answers when you need scaffolded workflows.

Practical advice

  • Use these techniques to reduce ambiguity and grading disputes, not to add complexity.
  • When a technique is new to you, start by copy-editing a known working example.
  • Preview multiple variants and test common student inputs before publishing.
