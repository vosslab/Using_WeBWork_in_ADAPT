This section is a practical ADAPT checklist for creating, testing, and assigning WeBWorK problems. It is written for copy-edit workflows: start from a working item, make small changes, and test until the student view and grading are stable.

Workflow overview

  1. Create or clone a question into your personal question area.
  2. Edit the code and metadata.
  3. Preview and revise until the prompt, answers, and scoring behave correctly.
  4. Add the question to an assignment.
  5. Reorder questions, adjust points, and publish.

Checklist for a new WeBWorK question

  • Start from a working PGML-first template (do not start from a blank file).
  • Keep PG setup minimal: values, context, answer objects, graders.
  • Write the prompt in PGML and attach answers to blanks.
  • Use a realistic life science story and keep the story stable across randomization.
  • Verify units, reporting expectations, and tolerance settings.
  • Preview multiple variants to ensure randomization stays valid.

Finding and reusing questions

  • Search for an existing question close to what you want.
  • Clone before editing so you keep the original as a reference.
  • Keep a small set of personal templates you trust and reuse them.

Adding questions to assignments

  • Add questions from multiple sources (search, shared banks, your question area).
  • Reorder questions to match your intended progression.
  • Adjust points only after you have confirmed the question behavior.
